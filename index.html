Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life. It has a diameter of about 12,742 kilometers and is composed of a dense iron-nickel core, a viscous mantle of silicate rock, and a thin, rigid crust. Earth's surface is approximately 71% water, primarily in the form of vast oceans, with the remainder consisting of continents and islands. The planet has a dynamic atmosphere, composed mainly of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), which plays a crucial role in regulating temperature and weather patterns. Earth's magnetic field, protects the surface from harmful solar radiation, while the ozone layer in the stratosphere shields life from ultraviolet rays.
