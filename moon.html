Moon

The Moon, Earth's only natural satellite, has a barren surface marked by craters, basins, and plains formed by billions of years of meteorite impacts. Its crust is primarily composed of silicate rock, with regions known as the lunar highlands being lighter in color and richer in plagioclase minerals, while the darker maria are basaltic plains formed by ancient volcanic activity.
